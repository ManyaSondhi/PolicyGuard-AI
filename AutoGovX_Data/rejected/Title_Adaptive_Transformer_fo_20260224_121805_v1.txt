Title: Adaptive Transformer for Medical Image Classification

Abstract:
This study presents a transformer-based deep learning model for medical image classification. The model was evaluated on a publicly available dermatology dataset consisting of 25,000 labeled samples.

Methodology:
We used stratified 5-fold cross validation to evaluate performance. The dataset was divided into training, validation, and test splits. Data augmentation techniques were applied to improve generalization.

Results:
The proposed model achieved 91.3% accuracy on the dataset. Precision and recall were balanced across classes. Compared to a ResNet baseline achieving 88.2% accuracy, our model showed improved feature representation and stability.

Discussion:
The results suggest improved contextual understanding of medical features. Error analysis indicates misclassifications occur primarily in visually similar lesion categories.

Conclusion:
This study demonstrates that transformer-based architectures can improve classification performance when trained on sufficiently large datasets.
